...To have been beared witness to the war's end was another. Florence Florenstine had been through much during his stay in Berenice. Moreso than some true participants of the war, even. Many times had he been within a residence that acted as a sanctuary for the war's victims, only to see them destroyed and their populace massacred. Many times had he sat out to do some good in this world as a counterbalance to all the evil occurring around him, only to be attacked and rendered helpless. So when the final self-serving soldiers of the war came together for one final battle, he couldn't help but see it come to a close. And what a battle it was. The doctor had never seen anything quite like it. Even with as much anger and hatred as he felt for these people, he couldn't help but find himself respecting such a showcase of human willpower and raw, unabated emotion. There was something so universally beautiful about it all that he'd forgotten to move from where he stood until the very moment everything went quiet. At the very least, the identity of the winner... or winners, that was... were known to him. By appearance, anyhow. And, for now, whatever wish they made seemed to have had little impact upon the world. A sigh of relief with strength never known to him was his reward for staying until the end. "...Which brings us to the present." Pencil in one hand, journal in the other, Florence found himself sitting at the very tail end of a wooden dock, just feet above the grand river that was the Nile. Had he been in the same place prior to the war, he'd have crossed his legs and kept them far from the river's surface. Now, he felt entirely unafraid of whatever could possibly be lurking underneath. Any sense of pride he pulled from this would quickly disappear. What had he done to be proud of? Being so shocked by this whole thing that he no longer felt common fear? Being so useless that his accomplishments amounted to nothing but innocent deaths?
