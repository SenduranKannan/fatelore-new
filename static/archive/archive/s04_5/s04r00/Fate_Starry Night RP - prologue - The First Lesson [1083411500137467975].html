October 12, 1999 Berenice Church, Berenice 'A summoning array, a catalyst, and a chant...That's it? That's all you need to perform the summoning ritual?' Sister Rosula didn't even bother to hide the skepticism in her tone. She reread her notes, which was filled with keywords that looked familiar, but the meaning of which were all but lost to her when she tried to string them together. She'd been on the phone for hours; never mind the exorbitant international calling fee, but her brain had also steadily become overwhelmed by all the information Father Andreas had unloaded during their first crash course into the world of mysticism. She tried not to cringe every time she let the word 'Magecraft' roll on her tongue. She also hadn't intended to sound so...underwhelmed, as she tried to absorb the information Father Andreas had just imparted about the Servant summoning ritual. 'Let me get this right,' she said, 'The summoning array...you can copy from a book. The chant...you can read off a notecard, so long as you are the one saying it. And the catalyst...it's not even required, just a nice to have.' 'That is correct, my good Sister. ' 'But it all sounds so...' 'Simple? Silly? Too good to be true?' 'Your words, Father.' 'Hahaha. The summoning ritual isn't exactly a well-kept secret, Sister.' Father Andreas' voice came through the phone, calm and collected as usual. 'Secrecy isn't the only source of a magus' power. In fact, lots of people have been exposed to these so-called mysteries, including, let's see... inventors? Engineers? Builders? Factory workers? Even stagehands and magician's assistants, writers and illustrators, they all dabbled in these "arts". And where do you think their accumulated knowledge coalesced? Books and works of art that are readily accessible to the general public. I don't suppose that even the Berenice Church has its own special collection of texts?' (edited)

Rosula nodded. 'Not the originals. But we do have a collection of holy scriptures, the earliest editions that exist in the country.' 'And there you have it. Many versions of the chant exist in verses and prose, and the summoning array is inherited through paintings and diagrams. You can even find vestiges of them in children's books, if you are willing to keep an open mind. Do you see now, how easy it is for someone to get their hands on these ingredients? Even a little kid is capable of doing it. In theory, that is.' Rosula was quiet for a moment, considering his words. 'I understand,' she finally said. 'It's just that, well, this is all a bit hard to take in. I've always thought of magic as something mystical, something grand and awe-inspiring. But from the way you've been explaining it to me, now it seems so...mundane. I'm sorry. I don't mean to doubt your words-' 'No need to apologize, Sister.' Andreas chuckled softly on the other end of the line. 'It's only natural to have doubts and questions about something that's so fundamentally different from what you're used to. But trust me when I say that this is because you are only seeing one part of it, and because I am delivering the information to you in bite-sized chunks. The summoning ritual is just the beginning; the true test lies in what comes next.' 'The Holy Grail War.' 'The summoning ritual is the price, and what comes out of the other end is your ticket. Only that you have no way of knowing whether it is a ticket to heaven or hell.' Again with the metaphors. 'I hope you aren't downplaying the significance of our work.' 'Our work has already been cut out for us. Might as well enjoy it while we can.'
9:32 AM
Rosula was suddenly very glad they were having this conversation over the phone. She wouldn't know what kind of expression to wear otherwise. There she was, doing her best to keep an open mind about magecraft, doing her utmost not to sound disrespectful, but Father Andreas almost seemed adamant on doing the very opposite. Make no mistake. Her brain was overwhelmed with information, but half of it were full of useless metaphors. And the scary thing was that she did not mind that one bit. Although Egypt had always been touted as the land of mysticism, Rosula had been brought up on a healthy diet of industrialization and a growing GDP supported by a booming tourism business. Even Berenice Island attributed the majority of its income to selling tickets, souvenirs, and booze. Having grown up far removed from the occult, popular media had always been the best source she had to lean on. Except that the magic on TV were always cast by wizards in sparkly robes or brewed by witches from their bubbly cauldrons. Her childhood self would no doubt be disappointed if those characters suddenly started calculating variables like a mathematician before casting their spells. Who'd have thought that 'real life magic' would be so dull? The more she talked with the mysterious priest across the Mediterranean Sea, the more she found herself disillusioned with the magic that had once fascinated her as a child. Father Andreas regarded magecraft with such nonchalance that he could've convinced her that he'd been talking about coupon clipping if he had wanted.

Was this really it? Just a few words printed in Times New Roman font on an A4-sized sheet of paper? Chalk and some chicken blood? Broken vases and old coins auctioned off of eBay? Perhaps these 'magicians' she would come to associate with wouldn't be so different from the star-struck youngsters who call upon their prayer circle, go to church, or sing as a sacrifice of praise unto the Lord so their crush would look at them differently the next day. She realized that their conversation had run its natural course. She had broken off mid-conversation, and from the faint sound of paper flipping on the other end of the line, Father Andreas had probably started sifting through his own notes to stave off his boredom. She did ask him to fax some records of the past Holy Grail Wars to read so that she would know what to expect, though he's also replied along the lines of getting her some of those 'funny anecdotes' of 'oddball Masters', so now she wasn't sure if she really wanted to read them. She cleared her throat. Best to get her money's worth out of this phone call, so that they didn't have to schedule too many sessions in the future. 'One more thing, Father.' 'I'm listening.' 'The Masters of the Holy Grail War. If it really is like you said, that anyone can become one by summoning a Servant, then-' 'Ah. I'd be careful about your wording, Sister. Anyone can attempt the ritual, yes. But only those born with magic circuits have a chance to successfully proceed. For everyone else, nothing would happen. The ritual would simply fail. They wouldn't even get a spark out of the array.' 'Of course. There's the catch.' Rosula rubbed her temples tiredly. 'Could you not have told me this earlier?' 'Well. I didn't think you'd be interested, that's all.' 'I can decide for myself, Father. I thought this was supposed to be a lesson.'

'I suppose I'm just not very good at teaching, then. My apologies.' Rosula could practically hear Father Andreas' smile through the phone. The man didn't sound apologetic at all. 'I'll try to be more diligent next time. It's only that, well, I didn't take you to be such an honor student. Gabriele never seemed wholly invested in my lectures when I still cared to give them. But maybe that's just a him problem, hmmmm.' 'If you are finished with your introspection, Father, I still haven't asked my question.' 'I'm right where you need me.' Rosula held back the urge to hang up the phone right then and there. 'The Masters,' she skimmed her own notes, 'what kind of people are they? Surely they'd have done their homework if they knew how to invoke the summoning ritual. Do they know that they are practically consenting to murder?' 'The mind of a magus works a bit differently than yours. To them, this isn't murder. It's a competition, with the Holy Grail as the prize.'' 'All to have their wish granted.' 'For the better part, yes. Desire is a powerful motivator. Even something as altruistic as world peace... which is the reason many wars were waged in the past, ironically. You would understand it better if you had a comparable wish of your own.' 'My only wish is for the casualties to be kept at a minimum.' 'We'll do our best, but no promises. It's not unheard of for more than half of the Masters to die in a single War. We should be thankful that Berenice isn't terribly populated.' 'I do not like what you are insinuating.'

'Neither do I, Sister.' Andreas sighed. 'But both mages and Servants are powerful, fickle beings. If you have any loved ones on the island, the most prudent thing to do would be asking them to take a vacation elsewhere for Christmas. Visit a relative, maybe? Plenty of places to go in Egypt. Or even overseas, if you can get your passports in time. Have they ever been to Budapest? Beautiful place, has a river running through the center too. Interesting fact, isn't it?' 'I will consider it.' Rosula's hands tightened around the phone. Father Andreas' words made her sick to her stomach. 'With all due respect, Father, I did not call you for vacation advice. I called you for information. If you have nothing else to add, I'm afraid I will have to end this call. If you want to give me follow-up lessons, please feel free to email me the time.' 'Wait, Sister, I'm not fini-' CLACK Dionysius Andreas blinked blankly as the flat dial tone buzzed in his ear. What a bizarre way to end the conversation. He hadn't even gotten to the most important part of their job yet, the damage control procedures! Suppose he'll make another call the next day? But first. He pulled out a pocket-sized address book and started flipping through the pages. The sheer negligence of Egypt's church sect had already gotten him concerned. There was no way Berenice could handle the Holy Grail War alone. As diligent as she was (and scarily so!), Sister Rosula's ignorance reflected poorly on the rest of her cohort. If they all wanted to get out of the War in one piece, then he would need much more capable hands. Someone with experience. Someone he could trust. Now where had he stashed his Motorola StarTAC™️...?
