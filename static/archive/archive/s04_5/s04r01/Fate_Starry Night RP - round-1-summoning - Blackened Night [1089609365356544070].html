Hassan Bazzi was a simple, innocuous man. 56 years old, with a long career in fruit selling and harvesting since he was 16. Was good at first, but eventually bills piled up. Was forced to take up a second job as a power station operative in his 30s, upping his total work hours to around 60 a week. Loving wife, but no kids. Doctors said that he was sterile. His life was dull. Boring, even. But Hassan didn't let it put him down. After all, Berenice was a beautiful place to live in. None of those noisy Aswan roads or confusing modern terms. He felt at peace there, accepted and part of a community. Even better was the fact that it was a holiday. Not of the state, but one that he had received for excellent service the past two weeks. His wife seemed unnaturally sleepy, so he decided to head over to the bar and meet up with some of his regular friends. "Well, guess they'll just let anyone in these days!" Kareem Laribi chuckled, sipping from his giant glass covered in beer and foam. He was one of Hassan's good friends, being the one who assisted him in plucking fruit during the harvest season. Both of them were cut from the same cloth, having had the same babysitter as kids. Kareem worked as a welder for some car company, meaning that he had to take the ferry to Aswan daily. Because of this, he had started to catch some modern traits that Hassan wasn't a fan of, but he wasn't nasty enough to put him down because of that. "Wa alaikum al-salam!" Hassan replied, greeting his brother and heading to the bartender to order a drink. The tavern was packed, which meant that at most it housed around a dozen or so people. Berenice was a small, isolated community. People either came and left or stayed for the remainder of their life. The building's structure reinforced that, being antiquated and made of stone. A certain rustic charm that only those of a certain age might have been able to appreciate.
