8:32 PM
Roland's watch had just ticked past midnight when they finally decided to make a pit stop. Lachlan was an impulsive driver, and neither of them trusted Bill enough to chauffeur them in the dead of night. Thus, the responsibility fell upon him to transport the three of them back to Wisconsin. And being the careful driver that he was, Roland demanded rest at reasonable hours. After all, no one would appreciate it if he fell asleep at the wheel. He had already been driving for a solid four hours since the excitement in the Colorado mountains; with the prospect of at least twelve more to go until they reached home, it was a smart move to stop and unwind for the night. The highway up northeast was almost deserted, but there were plenty of motel signs along the way. They pulled into the parking lot of one that looked relatively empty: fewer sets of eyes, fewer complications. The motel itself was nothing fancy, but it was adequate. They booked three rooms, one for each person in the group. For some reason, Lachlan had included Bill in the headcount, even though a Servant didn't need sleep, nor did they take up any room while in Spirit Form. In fact, Lachlan had insisted that Bill remain in Spirit Form while they negotiated with the front desk. Which made sense, given Bill's rather unorthodox appearance. But what didn't make sense was the fact that now they were a party of two, asking for three empty rooms. Thankfully, the staff didn't care enough to ask any questions. If they wanted an extra room, then they got an extra room. After all, an unoccupied room that had been paid for meant 100% unadulterated profit, as it required no cleaning the next day. The staff also didn't bother to check ID, especially not after Roland had slid them a generous tip in cash.
8:32 PM
Several minutes later and a hundred-odd dollars poorer, Roland shut the door to his room and finally reclaimed solitude. He was beyond exhausted, both mentally and physically. But the temptation to crash into bed would have to wait, as one look in the mirror and he was reminded of how desperately he needed a wash. Roland was a man of principle. He couldn't bring himself into bed without at least a quick rinse - even if said bed was a cheap, rickety motel bed whose sheets likely hadn't been changed for weeks. And so, human decency won out. The moment of intense inner struggle was over in a flash, and Roland hauled his tired ass into the shower. Just long enough to wash off the grimes from the day. He dried himself off and with a grimace, put his dirty clothes back on. The old shirt would have to do until he was back at the Dowling mansion. Somewhat clean and decently warm, he crawled into bed, intending to let sleep overtake him as he slipped under the sheets. But unfortunately, it seemed that sleep would evade him for a while longer. The moment the lights went off, thoughts began to swirl in his head. Roland quietly cursed himself. It seemed that the shower had done more than just wash off grime: it had also refreshed his mind, and now, in spite of his exhaustion, he found himself wide awake, doomed by intrusive thoughts that refused to be shut off. He tossed and turned, the sheets tangled around him, the mattress creaking beneath him. He stared at the ceiling, counted sheep, buried his head under the pillow, and tried to focus on his breathing. Nothing worked. The tick of his watch seemed to grow louder in the darkness, and he suppressed a savage urge to chuck it out of the window.
8:32 PM
Finally, in a fit of desperation, Roland tore himself out of bed and made his way to the balcony, a pack of smoke in hand. The chilly night air hit him as he stepped outside, but to hell with that, he was already wide awake anyway. Swiftly, he lit a cigarette and rolled it between his fingers, before taking a long drag. The nicotine kicked in almost immediately. He felt it reach his brains, a dizzy, hazy sort of feeling that relaxed him more than the shower had. He took another drag and stared out into the parking lot, lost in thought. He stood there for a while, until ash started to build up on the end of his cigarette. He pulled on it one last time, flicked the butt over the railing, and watched the ember snuff out in the darkness. Only then did it occur to him that if he didn't speak up now, the chance would be lost forever. Reluctantly, Roland took a deep breath and decided to acknowledge the redheaded phantom who had been hanging out on the neighboring balcony. 'So, I just wanted to know...were you planning on staying mute if I didn't say anything and went back to my room?' It didn't sit well with him that between him and Lachlan, he had to be the one to initiate this conversation.
