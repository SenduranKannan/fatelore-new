December 6th, 1999 - Ilkey, West Yorkshire. Somewhere in the outskirts of the town, 9:24 PM The sound of a neglected, creaky door opening right after a series of metallic clacks resounded throughout the stale, humid air of what was an old and dusty manor house. An old, and venerable indeed manor, that had not seen a recipient in a long, long time - And that even now it could vividly recall its days of yore that now lay behind it. Days in which its halls were flush with noise and buzzing with the restless activity of servants that day in and day out took care of it and of its owners that it so graciously offered its comfort, warmth and hospitality to. Once upon a time, that is. Now... It found itself vacant of any souls within its spacious halls and floors. The servants were long gone, and it's owners that it was used to seeing every summer... Vanished without a trace, never to be seen again. Yet it kept standing even so, maintaining its long vigil in the face of even the harshest of weathers, and the worst that nature could summon to try to tear it down, this monument to mankind's ingenuity. It did so in the hopes that the day would come that they would come back for it, that it could fulfill its purpose once more... Yet at last, finally, it would appear that its wish was granted and that someone had remembered it and came back, after all those years, someone had come back for it. With the front door opened now, fresh air flooded in through its numerous halls - uprooting the years of accumulated dust and horrid humid air that had accumulated within that was eating at its integrity like a horde of foul parasites. The manor accepted the old friend that had returned for it after all those years into its numerous branching halls, floors and spacious rooms with open arms. Even if it was not as pretty and clean as it once was like in its prime. It was overjoyed regardless to be of use once more to its owner that once more had returned.

The hardwood floor creaked softly underneath the weight of the first step that she took inside, as if the manor itself was welcoming her back audibly. There was barely any illumination provided to aid her eyes as she looked around - the night had snuffed any natural sources of light that would've provided it, and the moon had been obstructed by a thick veil of darkened clouds that were most certainly an ill omen that forewarned the residents of the town in advance for a downpour to occur at any moment. But none of that mattered much to her - She had no need to look around in the darkness for the light-switch as if this was her first visit into the venerable manor for she had already lived in it before. Or at the very least, that was what her implanted memories made her believe. With a flick of a switch that flanked the left side of the entrance with one of her iron digits, a series of lightbulbs sparked to life as electricity coursed through the cabling in the walls that laid dormant until now, and subsequently, through the filament within the bulbs suspended within argon gas. That chain of events caused the reception hall to in a near instant be fully illuminated and awash by a warm orange tint as if the sun itself was shining its rays in through the windows - Banishing the near depthless darkness that had taken root. Yet, from the numerous windows that flanked either side of Rosalie, and the grander one that was just up ahead in-between the main floor and the second one at the middle of the stairs - Only the empty darkness of the void devoid of any light or warmth could be observed outside of the manor. The interior of the manor was almost just as she recalled it being. Polished and exquisitely carved wood panels shaped in various shapes covered every inch of wall and pillar; arc and ceiling - And a single table upon a dusty carpet stood on its own in the center of it all. Upon its table cloth covered surface, a solitary bowl stood...

If she could've, she'd have taken a deep breath into her vacant lungs about now in order to calm her nerves whilst she looked dead ahead with unblinking, icy blue eyes that reflected a lack of warmth not too unlike that of a cold winter night. However those, those were both things that her body was incapable of. As she stood in place for a spare few moments to take in the old yet familiar scenery, the entire manor at that moment was plunged into complete silence with only the soft whistle of the wind brushing against the windows being the only solace in the somber atmosphere that had risen steadily within the entrance hall. Yet, in the back of Rosalie's mind - It was far from silent. Rosalie could hear the faint, yet distant sounds of echoing laughter of a young child alongside those of a pair of two distinctively more mature, yet just as jovial voices that were intelligible to her, yet she could discern the affection in them. Listening to them even as distant as they were made her feel comfort, even a slight sensation of inner warmth that normally should've been impossible for her machine body to parse. She concentrated on her hearing and focused, and focused as keenly and hard as she could at the voices. But no matter how much she did - The echoes of those voices were just too distant and for her to make out, and they were rapidly beginning to fade away as well. It was as if they were being drawn back to the past to which they belonged to, for the present was not their place, and the world not kind enough to let them linger for any much longer. Yet something down, deep down in the deepest recesses of Rosalie's being desired for them to stay, even for just a second more only so she could feel that comfort - That warmth! - Just for just a meager moment more! ... Silence With an outstretched arm that had moved on its own without her consent, Rosalie had found herself suddenly reaching out into nothingness - Her eyes as wide as they could ever be.
